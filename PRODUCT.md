# Product

## Register

product

## Users

A single power user — the developer/professional who owns this machine — juggling many concurrent work projects. They live in a keyboard-driven, multi-window desktop environment (editors, terminals, IDEs) and switch context between projects constantly. When they reach for Slate they are mid-task: capturing a decision, drafting a spec, pasting a code snippet, or pulling up an existing note for reference. Speed of capture and speed of retrieval matter more than onboarding or discoverability. They are fluent in tools like Obsidian, Linear, and their IDE, and expect the same fluency here.

## Product Purpose

Slate is a lightweight, native macOS app for taking and organizing notes across the many projects one person works on. It exists because general note apps are either too heavy and chrome-laden (maximalist Notion) or too sterile (corporate wikis), and neither feels fast or personal. Slate organizes notes by project — a project can hold a single note, many notes, or collections of notes — and supports rich text (bold, italic, highlight, color, code snippets, images). Success is when capturing or finding a note is faster than reaching for any alternative, and the app feels like an extension of the user's own workflow rather than a destination they visit.

## Brand Personality

Precise, engineered, quietly confident. Three words: **focused, fast, deliberate.** The voice is a sharp tool, not a friendly assistant — no hand-holding, no decoration for its own sake. Dark by default, the way a developer's environment is. Power-user energy: keyboard-first, dense where density earns its place, calm where the writing surface needs to breathe. One restrained signature detail carries the identity; everything else gets out of the way.

## Anti-references

- **Corporate / sterile** — Microsoft Office, Confluence, SharePoint. Gray, joyless, enterprise-committee design. Slate should never feel like software someone was forced to use.
- **Generic AI / SaaS** — the default purple-gradient, rounded-card, cream-background, hero-metric look that could be any startup. No gradient text, no glassmorphism-for-flavor, no identical card grids.
- Maximalist clutter (every toolbar and badge visible at once) and toy-like over-animation are also out, though they were secondary concerns.

## Design Principles

1. **The tool disappears into the task.** Chrome recedes; the note is the subject. Toolbars and panels earn their pixels or hide. Earned familiarity over invented affordances.
2. **Keyboard-first, fast.** Every primary action — new note, switch project, search, format — has a shortcut. Motion is 150–250ms and conveys state, never choreography. No page-load sequences.
3. **One signature, applied sparingly.** A single distinctive material/detail and a single accent make it unmistakably Slate. Distinctiveness lives in restraint, not in volume.
4. **Density where it helps, calm where it counts.** Project/note navigation can be dense and information-rich; the writing surface stays quiet and generous.
5. **Native and snappy.** It behaves like a real Mac app — instant launch, instant switch, respects system conventions — not a website in a window.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as the floor: body text ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1, including in both dark and light themes. Full keyboard navigation with visible focus states (a keyboard-first product cannot have keyboard-inaccessible controls). Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` with a crossfade/instant fallback for every animation. No known individual accommodations beyond standard best practice.
